# Philly Sprout — Problem Statement

> **Purpose:** Define the barriers to street tree planting in Philadelphia that Philly Sprout is positioned to address. Use this to guide landing page copy, product framing, and future features.

---

## The Opportunity

Philadelphia has **20% tree canopy coverage** — the lowest along the Northeast Corridor. PHS and the city aim to reach **30%**. The PHS TreeVitalize / Tree Tenders program offers **free** street trees, with volunteers and city contractors handling permits, concrete cutting, and planting. Yet most residents never participate.

---

## Four Barrier Categories

### 1. Program Awareness

**Problem:** Many residents don't know a free program exists. The program is invisible to most people.

**Philly Sprout's role:** Lead with "free" prominently. Make the value proposition impossible to miss.

---

### 2. Process Confusion

**Problem:** The application process is straightforward once you know the steps — but you must first **find your local PHS Tree Tenders group**. Most people never do. That group handles permits, pit preparation, and questions.

**Philly Sprout's role:** Act as a discovery on-ramp for people who don't know the Tree Tenders network exists. Simplify the path from "I want a tree" to "I'm connected to the right people."

---

### 3. Site Suitability Mysteries

**Problem:** Residents don't know if their block qualifies. The PHS checklist covers pit size, utility clearance, driveway proximity, and species fit — none of which are obvious to a layperson.

**Philly Sprout's role:** **Core AI value prop.** Pre-screen sites with vision AI against PHS criteria before anyone shows up with a shovel. Answer "Is my block a good candidate?" in seconds.

---

### 4. Misconceptions (the hardest barrier)

These are deeply held and worth addressing directly:

| Concern | Reality | Messaging approach |
|--------|---------|--------------------|
| **Sidewalk damage** | PHS plants trees sized for urban environments. Wrong species + poor maintenance caused past damage. A tree won't destroy pipes in good condition — most roots are within 18" of surface; Philly sewer laterals are ~6 ft down. | Acknowledge past problems; explain modern species selection and larger pit sizes significantly reduce risk. |
| **Who pays for damage** | Property owners are liable for sidewalk damage. This is a valid fear, not a myth. | Don't dismiss. Be honest: modern species selection and larger pit sizes reduce risk. |
| **Leaves & maintenance** | Most people who don't want trees cite leaves — big messy piles, inability to keep up with sweeping. WHYY | Real friction. Acknowledge it; offer practical tips (e.g., smaller species, leaf pickup schedules). |
| **Gentrification anxiety** | Some residents worry tree planting intensifies gentrification. Especially relevant in neighborhoods that need trees most. | Be aware; address only if appropriate for audience. |

---

## Philly Sprout's Positioning Shift

**Current:** Talks to people who already want a tree and need help with the process.

**Opportunity:** Convince the skeptical majority — people who haven't planted because they fear cost, hassle, or liability.

**Suggested message:**

> "Modern tree planting is nothing like what your block experienced 30 years ago. The wrong species were planted then. Today PHS selects trees that won't heave your sidewalk — and Philly Sprout helps verify your site qualifies before anyone shows up with a shovel."
